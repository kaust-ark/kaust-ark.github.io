What is ARK

ARK is an automated research harness that takes your idea to a submission-ready paper. It coordinates 8 specialized AI agents across experiments, writing, literature review, and figure generation — while you stay in control at every step.

8 AI Agents · 11+ Venue Presets · Human-in-the-Loop

Why ARK

How It Works

Three-Phase Pipeline

ARK iterates through research, development, and review until the paper meets quality targets.

1

Research Phase

Deep Research gathers background knowledge and literature survey.

2

Development Phase

Plan experiments, run on compute, analyze results, evaluate completeness, write initial draft.

3

Review Phase

Iterative paper improvement loop until the reviewer score hits the acceptance threshold.

Review Phase

Each iteration runs 5 steps

Compile LaTeX → PDF, page images
→
Review Score & identify issues
→
Plan Prioritize action items
→
Execute Research, experiment, write
→
Visualize Fix figures & recompile
↺

Meet the Team

8 Specialized AI Agents

Each agent is an expert in its domain, collaborating through shared state and structured handoffs.

Reviewer

Scores the paper and generates actionable improvement tasks.

Planner

Converts review feedback into a prioritized action plan.

Writer

Drafts and refines LaTeX sections based on plan tasks.

Experimenter

Runs experiments on remote compute and collects results.

Researcher

Surveys literature, identifies gaps, and gathers evidence.

Visualizer

Generates publication-quality figures with exact venue geometry.

Meta-Debugger

Detects stalls, diagnoses failures, and triggers self-repair.

Coder

Writes and debugs experiment code, scripts, and tooling.
